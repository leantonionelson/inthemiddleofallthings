# The Still Point Within

**Tags:** peace, recognition, awareness, consciousness, circumstances, inner-peace, realisation, witness, nature, stillness, boundless, wholeness, centre

---

We spend years trying to perfect the outer shape of life –
the right home, the right partner, the right path –
as though peace were something that could be constructed.
But peace does not depend on arrangement.
It depends on recognition.

You find peace not by rearranging the circumstances of your life,
but by realising who you are at the deepest level.

The circumstances are weather.
You are the sky.
The weather will always change –
but the sky, vast and unbothered, remains untouched.

Think of the thing you believe stands between you and peace.
A situation, a person, a lack, a loss.
Now imagine it resolved –
entirely, perfectly.
Would your mind then rest forever?
Or would it soon find another imperfection to fix?

The peace you chase externally
is only the echo of a peace that already exists within.
It cannot be earned;
it can only be remembered.

To realise who you are at the deepest level
is to remember that you are not the roles you play,
nor the stories you tell,
nor the shifting landscape of your emotions.
You are the awareness beneath them –
the silent witness
that watches everything come and go
without being diminished by any of it.

When you abide there,
life continues –
the joy, the pain, the change, the loss –
but the turbulence no longer defines you.
The surface may ripple,
but the depth remains calm.

Peace is not an outcome.
It is your nature.
It is what you were before the striving began,
and what you will be when it ends.

Stop rearranging the scenery.
Sit at the centre of the stage.
Remember the actor and the play
are both illuminated by the same light –
your consciousness.

You find peace
not by changing what happens,
but by remembering who it is happening to.
And when you know yourself as that –
still, boundless, whole –
peace ceases to be something you seek,
and becomes what you are.
