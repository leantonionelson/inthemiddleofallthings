Everything that has weight is finite.

It is the condition that gives life shape.

Without finitude, nothing differentiates.  
Without an end, no moment carries more force than another.

Time, as we experience it, is not a neutral backdrop.  
It is a pressure gradient.  
It compresses meaning by limiting duration.

This is why urgency exists.  
Why delay costs something.  
Why presence sharpens when time is felt.

In physics, time is inseparable from change.  
A system that does not change does not experience time.  
A system that cannot end does not move toward anything.

Human life is structured by this same rule.

Because time is limited, choice matters.  
Because energy is finite, attention becomes selective.  
Because bodies fail, priorities clarify.

Time does not make things meaningful by ending them, but by forcing choice.  
What cannot be postponed forever must finally be prioritised.

Mortality is not an interruption to meaning.  
It is the force that concentrates it.

Many philosophies attempt to soften this truth.  
To outthink it.  
To transcend it.

But finitude does not need consolation.  
It needs orientation.

The middle plays a decisive role here.

To be in the middle is to allow the knowledge of an ending  
to inform how weight is distributed now.

When time is ignored, life becomes diffuse.  
When time is feared, life becomes constricted.

When time is integrated, life becomes precise.

This is why regret is rarely about what happened.  
It is about misallocated energy.  
Attention spent where it did not belong.  
Commitments made without coherence.

The middle does not eliminate regret.  
It reduces its scale.

It keeps action proportional  
to what actually matters.

You do not need infinite time  
to live coherently.

You need enough clarity  
to place weight deliberately  
within the time you have.

Finitude does not ask you to rush.  
It asks you to choose.

Not perfectly.  
Not permanently.

But honestly.

A system that knows it will end  
can still remain whole.

And a life that accepts its limit  
can move through time  
without being scattered by it.

This is shape.
