Weight returns through attachment because attachment is consequential.

To love anything introduces asymmetry into the system.  
To care allows direction.  
To bind yourself to something finite accepts the possibility of loss.

Attachment is not primarily moral.  
It follows mechanics.

Every attachment creates gradient.  
Every gradient restores motion.

What nihilism suspends, love reactivates.

In a weightless world, nothing pulls.  
In an attached world, something does.

Time sharpens.  
Choice narrows.  
Presence acquires cost.

This is why love feels dangerous in flattened systems.  
It disrupts neutrality.  
It demands orientation.

That demand restores coherence.

A finite system is one that can be damaged.  
One that can be lost.  
One that cannot be replaced without consequence.

Only finite systems generate meaning.

This is structural.

In physics, systems that cannot lose energy cannot change state.  
In life, systems that cannot be lost cannot be valued.

Love introduces loss in advance.  
As condition rather than catastrophe.

Something matters because it can be lost,  
not because it will be preserved.

This is why attachment feels heavy.

Weight is not a flaw.  
It is a signal.

Loss, when it arrives, does not negate meaning.  
It completes it.

What breaks is not attachment itself,  
but the assumption that permanence was required.

Grief is evidence that love functioned.

The error is treating loss as something to avoid,  
rather than as the cost of coherence.

A system that avoids loss avoids pain.  
It also avoids depth.

A system that permits loss refines itself through consequence.

This is where the middle is tested.

To remain in the middle is to stay oriented within attachment.  
To hold position while allowing gravity to act.

Love pulls.  
Loss pulls harder.

The middle is not the absence of these forces.  
It is the point from which they can be borne  
without fragmentation.

Meaning does not return through certainty.

It returns through commitment.

Commitment shifts away from outcomes  
toward orientation under uncertainty.

The system moves again,  
weighted enough to produce direction.

And with weight, direction returns.

Real.  
Finite.  
Without guarantees.
