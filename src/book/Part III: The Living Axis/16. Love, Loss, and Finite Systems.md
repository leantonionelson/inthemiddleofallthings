Weight returns through attachment because it is consequential.

To love anything is to introduce asymmetry into the system.  
To care is to allow direction.  
To bind yourself to something finite is to accept that loss is now possible.

Attachment is not primarily moral.  
It behaves like physics.

Every attachment creates gradient.  
Every gradient reintroduces motion.

What nihilism suspends, love reactivates.

In a weightless world, nothing pulls.  
In an attached world, something does.

Time sharpens.  
Choice narrows.  
Presence becomes costly.

This is why love feels dangerous in flattened systems.  
It breaks neutrality.  
It demands orientation.

But this demand is precisely what restores coherence.

A finite system is one that can be damaged.  
One that can be lost.  
One that cannot be replaced without consequence.

Only finite systems generate meaning.

This is a structural claim.

In physics, systems that cannot lose energy cannot change state.  
In life, systems that cannot be lost cannot be valued.

Love introduces loss in advance.  
Not as tragedy,  
but as condition.

You do not wait to lose something in order for it to matter.  
It matters because it *could* be lost.

This is why attachment feels heavy.  
It is.

Weight is not a flaw.  
It is the signal that the system is alive.

Loss, when it arrives, does not negate meaning.  
It completes it.

What breaks is not the attachment,  
but the illusion that permanence was required.

Grief is evidence that love functioned.

The mistake is to treat loss as something to be avoided,  
rather than as the cost of coherence.

A system without loss avoids pain,  
but also avoids depth.

A system that allows loss refines itself through consequence.

This is where the middle is tested.

To be in the middle is to remain oriented within attachment.

It is to remain oriented  
while allowing gravity to act.

Love pulls.  
Loss pulls harder.

The middle is not the absence of these forces,  
but the point from which they can be borne  
without fragmentation.

This is why meaning does not return through certainty.  
It returns through commitment.

Commitment shifts from outcomes to orientation under uncertainty.

The system moves again, weighted enough to produce direction.

And with weight comes direction.

Real.  
Without guarantees or permanence.
