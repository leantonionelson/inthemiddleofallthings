# Lens

**Tags:** consciousness, awareness, identity, perception, unity, separation, wholeness, mirror, fractal, becoming, love, lens, reflection, transformation, self-knowledge, recognition

---

It began before beginnings were possible.
Before there were edges to hold a thought or a sky to hold a name.

There was only the Whole.
The **is**.
The **all**.
A single, complete awareness folded into itself like a perfect mirror.

And if you listen closely, you might feel the faint echo of this in yourself –
that quiet knowing that precedes every word you've ever spoken.

But even a perfect mirror, to know itself, must reflect something.
It must see its own face from another angle.
It must create a vantage point from which to behold its own vastness.

So the Whole did something both simple and impossible.
It turned outward – and in doing so, fractured.

Not violently.
Not tragically.
Just with a soft, inevitable curiosity.

**One became Many.**
The lens shattered.
The fractal broke itself into an infinity of pieces.

And one of those pieces is reading this now.

When the fragment first opened its eyes, it felt the shock of separation.
The first breath of **I**.
A boundary forming where no boundary had ever been.

You know this feeling.
You've lived it many times –
in birth,
in heartbreak,
in moments when life pulled something from you
that you thought you could not live without.

That first "I" did not arrive gently.
Existence flooded it.
Raw, bright, unfiltered.
Too much to hold all at once.

And so the mind did what all minds do when the world arrives faster than they can bear:

It narrowed.
It focused.
It declared, *"I am this."*

This was the first survival.
The first identity.
The first "truth."
And with it, inevitably –
*I am not that.*

You know this move too.
Every time you define yourself,
a shadow is cast behind you.
Every certainty excludes another possibility.
Every safety creates a blind spot.

Here, the ancient story becomes your story.

Cultures formed.
Beliefs hardened.
Patterns of survival became inherited wisdom.
These patterns did what they were meant to do –
they kept you alive,
kept you oriented,
gave you a place to stand.

But anything held too long begins to rust.

The lens that once protected you grows heavy.
The world narrows.
The unknown begins to look like threat.
Difference begins to feel like danger.
Love becomes harder to absorb.
Even kindness feels suspicious when the lens has been weighted in fear.

This is how the ancient "hells" were born.
Not below us.
Not after us.
*Within us.*
States where the mind cannot receive what it desperately longs for.
Where love is misread as intrusion,
beauty as noise,
and every other person becomes a distortion in the glass.

You have felt this too –
those moments where nothing good can reach you,
even when it tries.
Not because it is absent,
but because the lens cannot take it in.

But the universe – your universe – does not leave you here.

It moves in spirals.
It repeats, yes,
but always with difference.
Always with invitation.

So it sends you the very thing your old lens cannot welcome:
the other.
The opposite.
The unexpected.
The shattering.

Someone or something that cracks your certainty,
that makes you question your inheritance,
your posture,
your story about yourself.

This breaking never feels divine.
It feels like loss.
Like betrayal.
Like the floor falling away.

But look back, and you will see –
every rupture returned you to possibility.
Every antithesis drew you closer to your own centre.

This is the turning point.
The beginning of the middle.

The place where the lens stops being an identity
and begins to become a tool.
A living, shifting, open surface through which the world can flow again.

This is where you learn to look in,
not to retreat,
but to recognise the whole fractal pattern in yourself.
The same symmetry the Whole once held as its only truth.

And then to look out
and see that symmetry mirrored in every face you meet –
even the ones that challenge you the most.

Especially those.

This is the quiet work.
The real work.
Not self-improvement.
Not purity.
Not transcendence as escape.

Just clearing the lens.

Letting it become balanced enough
that love is no longer something you must manufacture or perform –
but something that simply flows when perception is unobstructed.

In this state, "love" is not an emotion.
It is the natural behaviour of a lens that has learned to see clearly again.
A recognition.
A remembering.

It is the moment you look at another and feel,
without analysis or effort:

*Ah.
There you are.
Me, too.*

And if this resonates, even slightly,
then the telling is already doing its work.

Because this isn't a story about origins.
It's a reminder of where you already stand:

Between the One and the Many.
Between the mirror and the face.
Between the remembering and the becoming.
Always in the middle.
Always at the axis.
Always returning to yourself through the eyes of another.
